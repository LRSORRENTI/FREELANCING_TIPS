FREELANCING GUIDELINES

There are three points of liability

  1. The first is transaction security.
  2. The second is uptime.
  3. The third, and final is not meeting benefit targets.

Transaction Security

If you're dealing with any sensitive info like credit card numbers, payments or Healthcare info, then you can get sued out the rear if a breach is due to faulty code.

There are actually legal standards for maintaining any of this info and/or transacting it.

Uptime

If you not only build a website but maintain it under some sort of agreement AND if the customer has no direct way to maintain it themselves in an emergency, you may be on the hook for lost revenues caused by an outage.

Not meeting benefits targets

If you tell a customer that redesigning their website will get them more direct sales, and if sales maintain or decrease, then you possibly could be liable for lying about the services or products rendered.
